A better way to control digital cameras

Cam is an app that can remotely control your camera.

Why another camera controller app?

In my years of using a DSLR, I've noticed two common problems with 3rd-party controller apps:

  • Not enough features, not enough customization, too locked down
  • The app is often buggy, broken, and slow

How do you fix these problems?

Cam takes some inspiration from the videogames industry, and lets you have full modding capabilities over the app. This gives you the freedom to automate obscure workflows, and suite the app for your needs.

A separate project closely tied to this one is vcam a reimplemented camera that can perfectly spoof Canon's own software. It's provided freely for anybody who wants to to use it to make camera tools.

Features:

  • Support for Canon EOS cameras
  • Liveview with zoom
  • Profiles - preconfigured settings & parameters for each camera / workflow
  • Rich scripting API - add buttons and scripts to the app to do whatever you want
  • Get & Set ISO, aperture, shutter speed, white balance
  • Take pictures and BULB shots
  • Preview and download captured images
  • Manually and precisely focus lens
  • File manager and photo gallery
  • Magic Lantern integration
  • Capture and preview photos

Planned Features:

  • Support for Sony cameras
  • Use Android device as external microphone
  • GPS & clock sync
  • Customizable Zebras/histogram overlays
